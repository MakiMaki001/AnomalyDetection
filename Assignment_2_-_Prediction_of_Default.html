Paper Reading: Feature engineering strategies for credit card fraud detection.

Analysis of paper:

The paper goes into depth in the benefits of detecting credit card fraud through the aggregation of key features. The authors relied on various versions of Decision Trees, Logistic regression, Random Forest, and Bayesian models in order to judge accuracy of detecting credit card fraud. The way this was judged was by using these algorithims against the raw dataset, with aggregated features, and periodic features. The study showed the aggregating features performed better, particularly with the time based aggregations. Instead of taking one transaction at a time, they grouped transactions based on the time of day that they happen.

In order to capture the frequency of transactions not just in one day, the time frames of the periodic breakdown were expanded to 1 week. This was an excellent approach which allowed the model to really identify outliers in unusual transactions done by customer, daily transactions would have shown up consistently 7 times in the period, while weekly transactions would show once. The effectivness of this approach can be expanded to even larger time frames, as customers can have monthly transactions as well that could be outliers. The conclusion of the paper was that aggregated features did, in fact, perform better at predicting fraudulent transactions than raw features with no periodic elements included.
